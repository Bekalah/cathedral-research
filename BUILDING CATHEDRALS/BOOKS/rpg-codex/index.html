üé™ Design Philosophy

RPG Codex draws inspiration from the greatest non-D&D fantasy RPGs to create a realistic, narrative-driven tabletop experience. Unlike traditional fantasy systems, we focus on:

üß† Psychological Depth (Disco Elysium)

Character skills include Inland Empire, Rhetoric, Authority, and Empathy. Internal monologue mechanics drive character development.

‚öîÔ∏è Gritty Realism (The Witcher 3)

Combat is deadly and tactical. Preparation, alchemy, and investigation matter more than raw power.

üèõÔ∏è Deep Lore (Pillars of Eternity)

Original pantheon, soul mechanics, and reincarnation cycles. Every choice echoes across lifetimes.

üåë Moral Ambiguity (Tyranny)

No clear good vs evil. Players navigate complex political situations where every faction has valid points.

üìã Step 1: Background & Origin

Choose your character's origin story:

  • Noble Exile: Politics forced you from power (+2 Authority, +1 Rhetoric)
  • Street Scholar: Learned from life's hard lessons (+2 Streetwise, +1 Inland Empire)
  • Monastery Dropout: Left religious life for adventure (+2 Lore, +1 Empathy)
  • Mercenary Veteran: Survived countless battles (+2 Combat, +1 Endurance)
  • Traveling Merchant: Knows the value of everything (+2 Commerce, +1 Persuasion)
  • Hedge Witch: Practices folk magic (+2 Nature Lore, +1 Alchemy)

üß† Step 2: Mental Attributes

Distribute 20 points among mental attributes (2-6 range):

  • Intellect: Logic, reasoning, pattern recognition
  • Psyche: Willpower, mental fortitude, soul strength
  • Physique: Body strength, endurance, physical presence
  • Motorics: Coordination, reflexes, fine motor skills

üé≠ Step 3: Psychological Skills

Each attribute governs 6 skills. Invest 30 points (0-5 range):

Intellect Skills:

  • Logic: Deduction and reasoning
  • Encyclopedia: Factual knowledge
  • Rhetoric: Persuasive argument
  • Drama: Acting and deception
  • Conceptualization: Abstract thinking
  • Visual Calculus: Spatial reasoning
